Extension Notes: How to keep your computer clean

by Laura L. Sant Franklin County Extension Educator

Do you wash your hands when you get to work? Consider what you touched before you get to work and how contaminated your hands may be. Then when you get to work, you may grab a beverage or turn on your computer and get to work. Bacteria and germs on your hands will be transferred to whatever you touch if you donâ€™t wash your hands first. Other people may also contaminate your workstation if they use your computer or sneeze/cough near your desk.

Do you wash your hands before eating at your desk? The problem with this is germs on your workstation will end up on your hands. From your hands, the germs transfer to your food which you eat. This is how bacteria and germs enter your body and make you sick. Touching around your eyes or nose may also introduce bacteria and germs into your body. To prevent this, consider washing your hands, desk and computer every morning before you start working.
